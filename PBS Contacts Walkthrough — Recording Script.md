# PBS Contacts Module — Recording Script

**Target length:** ~8–10 minutes
**Recommended setup:** OBS or Loom, 1920×1080, full app window, no other tabs visible. Mic close, room quiet. Have one real-looking test contact already created so the detail view has data to show (a few comm log entries help too).
**Voice tone:** friendly, plain-spoken — talk like a coworker showing someone the ropes, not a corporate tutorial.

Stage directions in *italics*. Spoken lines in regular text. Approximate timestamps in **bold** at section starts.

---

## Scene 1 — Intro (0:00 → 0:25)

*Open with the PBS dashboard. Cursor relaxed in the centre.*

> "Hey, in this quick walkthrough I'll show you how to use the Contacts module in PBS. We'll cover finding contacts, adding new ones — both people and companies — using the detail view to log calls and notes, and the few power features that come in handy day-to-day. Let's jump in."

*Click **Contacts** in the left nav.*

---

## Scene 2 — The list view & tabs (0:25 → 1:15)

*Land on the Contacts page. Pause one beat so the layout reads.*

> "When you open Contacts, you land on the **Individuals** tab. The count next to it shows how many people you have. Right next to it is the **Companies** tab — same idea but for businesses. And on the far right is a **Settings** tab where you can import or export your whole list."

*Hover over each tab in turn, then click **Companies** briefly, then back to **Individuals**.*

> "The columns are the basics — name, who it's assigned to, phone, cell, email, address, city and state, the stage they're in, and when you created them. Every name in the list is clickable — that opens the contact's detail page, which we'll get to in a minute."

*Hover the **Name** column header.*

> "Any of these column headers can be clicked to sort the table — click once for ascending, again for descending. The default sort is last name, A to Z."

---

## Scene 3 — Searching, filtering, paging (1:15 → 1:55)

*Click into the search box.*

> "The search box across the top searches name, email, phone, and city all at once. Type a few letters and the list narrows down as you go."

*Type "smi" — let results appear. Pause one beat. Clear the box.*

> "When your list grows past 200, the pagination shows up at the bottom. Use the arrows to page through, or jump straight to a numbered page. The little **'X to Y of Z'** counter tells you exactly where you are."

*Scroll to bottom briefly to show pager controls if available.*

---

## Scene 4 — Adding an individual contact (1:55 → 3:25)

*Scroll back up. Click **+ Add Contact** in the top right.*

> "To add a new person, click **+ Add Contact**. This opens a modal — no separate page, just fill it in and save."

*Modal opens. Slow cursor through the top of the form.*

> "**Last Name** is the only required field — well, technically you need either first or last, but in practice you'll usually want both. Fill in **First Name**, **Last Name**, and if it's a couple, **Spouse or Partner First and Last** — that way both names are searchable on the same record."

*Type a sample contact, e.g. "John Smith" / "Mary Smith".*

> "Phone, cell, email — standard stuff. If they have more than one phone or email, drop the extras into **Additional Phones** or **Additional Emails**, one per line."

*Scroll down in the modal.*

> "Below that you've got the address fields — street, city, state, zip. Below that, **Assigned To** lets you assign this contact to a salesperson or estimator. And **Consultation Type** flags whether this person is in for a design consult, an estimate, or neither."

*Fill in a few. Scroll to Workflow section.*

> "**Stage** is how this contact moves through the pipeline — New Lead, Warm Lead, Consultation, Quoted, Won, Lost, Nurture. There are a couple of import-only stages — BT Import and GHL Import — used by sync tools, you usually won't pick those manually."

*Pull down the Stage dropdown to show the options, then pick **New Lead**.*

> "**Notes** is freeform — any context that helps you remember this person. **Project Description** is more specific — what's the actual job they're interested in?"

*Scroll to the Marketing section.*

> "The Marketing section captures attribution — **Contact Type** (residential, commercial, or public works), **Source Type** (Google, referral, etc.), **Campaign** for ad-spend tracking, and **Source Origin** for how they actually found you in their own words."

> "And the **Interested In** dropdowns — three slots — let you tag what they're shopping for: pavers, walls, lighting, pool, the whole list. This is what makes 'show me everyone interested in fire pits' a one-click filter later on."

*Scroll to bottom.*

> "**Date of Birth** if you collect it, **Call Center Notes** if there's anything specific your CSR team needs to know — then **Add Contact** at the bottom and you're done."

*Click **Add Contact**. Land back on the list with the new row visible.*

---

## Scene 5 — Adding a company (3:25 → 4:10)

*Click **Companies** tab, then **+ Add Company**.*

> "Adding a company works the same way but with a couple of differences. **Company Name** is required. You've got Company Address, Main Phone, Main Email, and Website."

*Fill in a sample, e.g. "Acme Landscaping LLC".*

> "Where companies differ is the **Company Contacts** section. Click **+ Add Person** and you can attach individual people to this company — the owner, an office manager, an AP person — each with their own first name, last name, title, phone, and email. Add as many as you need."

*Click **+ Add Person** once, fill in one example. Then add another with **+ Add Person** to show repeatability.*

> "The rest of the form mirrors individual contacts — assignment, stage, marketing fields. Save the same way."

*Click **Add Company**. Back to list.*

---

## Scene 6 — Detail view, the big picture (4:10 → 4:40)

*From the Individuals tab, click the test contact you created earlier.*

> "Click any name and you land on the contact's detail page. This is where the real work happens — three columns. Left has the identity card and meta. The middle is your communication log. The right is the pipeline and recent activity. Let me walk through each."

*Brief cursor sweep across all three columns.*

---

## Scene 7 — Identity card + Stage (4:40 → 5:25)

*Focus left column, top.*

> "Top-left is the identity card — initials avatar, full name, and three action icons in the corner: **Edit** opens the same modal we just saw, **Convert to Company** turns this person into a company record if you find out they're actually a business, and **Delete** removes them. We'll come back to those in a minute."

> "Below that, all the contact info — spouse, address, phone, cell, email — and every phone and email is a clickable link. Tap email, your mail client opens. Tap phone, on mobile it dials."

*Click the email link to demonstrate (don't actually send — just show it triggers). Cancel out.*

*Scroll down to the Stage card.*

> "Below that is the **Stage** selector — same nine options as the create form. Change the stage here and it saves immediately, AND it auto-logs a stage-change entry into the communication log so you have a record of when this person moved through the pipeline."

*Change Stage once to demo, then change back.*

---

## Scene 8 — The four info tabs (5:25 → 6:15)

*Click **More Info** tab.*

> "Underneath are four tabs. **More Info** holds the secondary details — additional emails and phones, assigned to, consultation type, date of birth, and the project description."

*Click **Marketing**.*

> "**Marketing** shows the attribution and the interest tags — what they're shopping for, where they came from."

*Click **DND**.*

> "**DND** — Do Not Disturb — is important. Three toggles: phone, email, SMS. If a customer asks you to stop calling, flip the phone toggle. If they unsubscribe from email, flip email. Same for text. These save instantly and a warning banner appears on the contact so nobody on your team accidentally reaches out on a blocked channel."

*Click each toggle on then off to show the warning banner appear/disappear.*

*Click **Tags**.*

> "**Tags** lets you add freeform labels — 'VIP', 'Repeat Customer', whatever helps you. Type a tag, hit Enter, and it's added. Click the × to remove."

*Add "VIP" tag, then remove it.*

---

## Scene 9 — Communication log (6:15 → 7:15)

*Focus middle column.*

> "The middle column is the communication log — every note, call, email, and text logged on this contact, in time order. System events like stage changes show up here too with a little icon, so you've got one chronological story of the relationship."

*Scroll the log briefly if there are entries.*

> "At the bottom, four type buttons — **Note**, **Call**, **Email**, **Text** — pick the type of entry you want to log. If it's a call, email, or text, you also pick **inbound** or **outbound**."

*Click **Call**. Click **outbound**.*

> "Type what happened — 'left voicemail about backyard estimate' — and hit Send."

*Type a short example, click **Send**. The entry appears.*

> "And there it is. Same flow for emails and texts when you want to log what was said outside the app."

---

## Scene 10 — Right column (7:15 → 7:55)

*Move to right column.*

> "Right column has three small cards. **Pipeline** shows all nine stages stacked — click any stage button to move the contact there. The current stage is highlighted, past stages are grayed out, future stages are clickable. Same thing as the stage dropdown but more visual."

> "**Recent Activity** shows the last few stage changes and the most recent note, call, email, or text — a quick 'where are we' summary."

> "And **Opportunities** has a green button — **+ Add as Opportunity**. Click that and PBS creates a linked Opportunity record over in the Opportunities module, with this contact as the client. Once linked, the **Linked Opportunity** field on the left column shows the connection both ways."

*Hover the **+ Add as Opportunity** button — don't click unless you want to demo it.*

---

## Scene 11 — Convert to Company & Delete (7:55 → 8:25)

*Scroll back to the top of the left column. Hover the **Convert to Company** icon.*

> "Two more things worth knowing. **Convert to Company** — if you discover the John Smith you've been logging calls with is actually the owner of Smith Excavation, this button rolls all his contact data and all his communications over into a new company record. One click, no copying and pasting."

*Hover **Delete**.*

> "And **Delete** removes the contact and every communication on it. There's a confirmation step so you can't trigger it by accident."

---

## Scene 12 — Settings — Import & Export (8:25 → 9:00)

*Navigate back to **Contacts** in the nav. Click the **Settings** tab.*

> "Last stop — the Settings tab. **Import** lets you bulk-upload contacts from a file, useful when you're moving from a spreadsheet or another CRM. **Export** downloads everything in your contacts list — handy for backups or for handing off to an outside team."

*Briefly hover each button (don't actually trigger an import).*

---

## Scene 13 — Wrap (9:00 → 9:30)

*Land back on the Individuals list.*

> "That's the Contacts module. Three things to remember: search the list when you're hunting for someone, use the detail view to log every call and note so you have a real record, and lean on the stage pipeline and DND toggles to keep your team coordinated. Any questions, hit Sam in the top bar — he can help. Thanks for watching."

*End screen / fade.*

## Post-production notes

- **Subtitles**: enable auto-captions and clean up the technical terms (PBS, GHL, DND).
- **Highlights**: a brief yellow ring or zoom on the active element every time the cursor lands on something the narration mentions.
- **Cuts**: trim every "uh" and every cursor-fumble — the cleaner the cursor, the more professional the tutorial reads.
- **Music**: optional — quiet instrumental at -25dB or lower if used at all.
- **Outro**: optional 2-second PBS logo card.

## Field-checklist (use this while recording)

- [ ] Test contact has at least 2 stage changes and 3 communication entries before recording
- [ ] Browser zoom set to 100% so UI matches everyone else
- [ ] No personal data on screen (real customer names, emails, phone numbers)
- [ ] No browser bookmarks bar showing
- [ ] OS notifications muted
- [ ] Slack / email / SMS apps quit
